Joshua Salako

M.Sc. Graduate, Google DeepMind Scholar

Stellenbosch University & AIMS South Africa

salakojoshua1234[at]gmail[dot]com

Hi 👋🏿 I recently completed my master's in AI for Science at AIMS South Africa and Stellenbosch University, graduating with distinction. I am grateful to have been supervised by Dr Lorène Jeantet. I work on machine learning for ecology—mostly computer vision for making sense of camera-trap data.

For my master's, I taught models to spot endangered Western Leopard Toads across 1.2 million camera-trap images, then used those detections to test whether under-road tunnels actually help the toads avoid roadkill. A very interesting project, and perhaps the most consequential game of “find the toad” I will ever play. I am now working with the Machine Learning for Ecology group at AIMS and looking for PhD opportunities—very open to conversations!

I am also curious about agentic systems. Alongside research, I am an AI/ML Engineer at Rio Eigen (industry experience pays too—quite literally). Away from models, you will usually find me behind a camera or somewhere inside a good playlist.
